Party Loot Ledger – Help

Track your party’s loot with trade value, tier, status, and notes. Use the four tabs to manage items, roll random hoards, set market conditions, and track factions.

Ledger

Add items manually: name, tier (T0 Mundane through T5 Legendary), base value in gp, quantity, and status (Holding, For Sale, Sold, Leverage). Use notes for provenance or faction interest. Click a status in the table to cycle it; use × to remove an item. Active Holdings Total sums only items not marked Sold.

Loot Roller

Choose a tier system: Standard 5e (CR-based) uses encounter difficulty (Low/Mid/High/Deadly) and hoard type for classic 5e loot. Custom (leverage / trade tiers) uses the same T0–T5 tiers as the ledger plus a market context (open market, guild, faction deal, black market, political). Custom tables reflect leverage ≠ rarity: fence tax, faction currency, holding value, provenance, and legal jurisdiction. Roll to get random loot; use Add All to Ledger or Re-roll.

Market

Market Conditions affect how you might roleplay selling (e.g. City Under Siege, Trade Festival). Randomize to set two conditions, or Clear to remove them. The Leverage Calculator shows a 0–100 leverage score by item tier and buyer type (open market, guild, noble, black market, allied faction)—useful for haggling or faction deals.

Factions

View built-in factions (Harpers, Zhentarim, etc.) and add your own. For each faction you can set alignment (Ally, Neutral, Hostile, Unknown) and what they want to buy—handy when matching ledger items to buyers.

Sync & data

The bar under the header shows whether the ledger is synced. Data is saved in your browser; if the hub’s shared storage is available, the same ledger can be used across devices.

Appearance

Use Hub style to match the rest of the D&D Tools Hub (and light/dark theme). Use Parchment for a darker, parchment look. Your choice is remembered.
